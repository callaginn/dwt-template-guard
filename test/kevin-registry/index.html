About the Registry

The National Registry of Ducks Named Kevin (NRDNK) was established pursuant to Executive Order 13,242 — Ensuring the Orderly Identification and Tracking of Waterfowl Named Kevin — signed on March 14, 2003, at 9:17 AM, after a brief interagency debate over whether “Kevin” counts as infrastructure. The Registry operates under the authority of the U.S. Department of Waterfowl Nomenclature and is funded through the annual Migratory Bird Tax, plus whatever coins people throw into fountains with Kevins in them (these are, regrettably, federal revenue).

All ducks named Kevin within U.S. territorial waters and their adjacent ponds, lakes, canals, decorative fountains, and the koi pond at the Reston Town Center must be registered within 90 days of naming. Failure to comply is a civil infraction subject to penalties of up to $500 per unregistered Kevin and mandatory enrollment in a half-day Waterfowl Awareness Seminar (100% attendance, 0% satisfaction, 110% folding chairs). Please note: the Seminar is not about awareness for ducks. It is awareness of ducks.

Frequently Cited Regulations

  • § 4.01 — A duck may not be named Kevin retroactively. The name must be given before registration, not after. (The Registry has received multiple applications where the name was assigned “just now” in the margin. This is not a loophole; it is handwriting.)
  • § 7.12 — A single body of water may not contain more than four (4) Kevins at any time without a Density Variance Permit (Form NRDNK-DV1). This limit was established following the Prospect Park Incident of 2014, which the Registry describes as “an avoidable Kevin clustering event.”
  • § 9.03(b) — Bread consumption events must be logged within 48 hours. The Registry does not accept estimates, guesses, or “vibes.” We do accept vibes on Form NRDNK-VIBE-2, but it’s currently backordered pending a vibes shortage.
